# Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (Template)

> **⚠ Template, not a finalised contract.**
>
> Sign-off by a Bangladesh-licensed lawyer is required before first use. The structure below covers the substantive points a bank's legal team will look for in an AML-platform pilot context, but specific clauses around regulator-disclosure carve-outs, term length, and remedies must be checked against current Bangladesh law (Contract Act 1872, Digital Security Act 2018, and any BFIU or Bangladesh Bank circulars governing vendor-disclosure protections in effect at signing). Sections marked `[LAWYER REVIEW]` are most likely to need rewriting; the rest is structural and should hold.
>
> Sign this **before** the Pilot Agreement, not at the same time. The pilot agreement references this NDA in Schedule C; that reference fails if the NDA hasn't been executed.
>
> Designed to fit on a single printed page (A4) when rendered to PDF.

---

## Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("**Agreement**") is entered into on **[Effective Date]** between:

**ENSO INTELLIGENCE INC.**, having its operational office at [Office Address, Dhaka, Bangladesh] ("**Kestrel**"); and

**[BANK NAME]**, having its registered office at [Bank Registered Address] ("**Counterparty**"),

each a "**Party**" and together the "**Parties**".

---

## 1. Purpose

The Parties wish to discuss and evaluate a potential business relationship in connection with Kestrel's financial-crime intelligence platform and the Counterparty's AML and CFT operations (the "**Purpose**"). In the course of these discussions each Party may share confidential information with the other.

## 2. Confidential Information

"**Confidential Information**" means any information disclosed by one Party (the "**Discloser**") to the other (the "**Recipient**") in connection with the Purpose, whether disclosed orally, in writing, electronically, or by demonstration, that a reasonable business person would regard as confidential, including without limitation:

- (a) on Kestrel's side: platform architecture, source code, prompt content, pricing, customer lists, roadmap, security posture, RLS policies, AI red-team corpus, deployment topology;
- (b) on the Counterparty's side: existing AML and CFT stack, book size, transaction volumes, customer demographics, internal compliance metrics, names of senior bank personnel, regulatory feedback received from BFIU or Bangladesh Bank, the contents of any STRs or CTRs shown during demonstrations;
- (c) on either side: the existence and contents of these discussions, the negotiated price, success-criteria results, and the contents of any pilot or subscription agreement that may follow.

Confidential Information **need not be marked or labelled as confidential** to be protected under this Agreement.

## 3. Exclusions

This Agreement does not apply to information that:
- (a) is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Recipient;
- (b) was already in the Recipient's possession, free of any duty of confidentiality, before disclosure by the Discloser;
- (c) is independently developed by the Recipient without use of or reference to the Discloser's Confidential Information; or
- (d) is lawfully received from a third party who is not bound by an obligation of confidentiality to the Discloser.

## 4. Permitted use and disclosure

The Recipient shall:
- use the Confidential Information **solely for the Purpose**, and for no other purpose;
- protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of similar sensitivity, and in no event less than reasonable care;
- limit access to the Confidential Information to its directors, employees, advisors, contractors, and (where applicable) regulators who have a need-to-know in connection with the Purpose and are themselves bound by written or professional obligations of confidentiality at least as protective as this Agreement;
- not reverse-engineer, decompile, or attempt to derive the source of any Confidential Information;
- not disclose the Confidential Information to any other third party without the Discloser's prior written consent.

## 5. Compelled disclosure

`[LAWYER REVIEW]` — The Recipient may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by applicable law, regulation, court order, or by a request from a competent regulatory authority (including without limitation the Bangladesh Financial Intelligence Unit, Bangladesh Bank, or a court of competent jurisdiction). The Recipient shall, to the extent legally permitted, provide the Discloser with prompt prior written notice so the Discloser can seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy, and shall co-operate reasonably in any such effort.

## 6. Term

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for **three (3) years**, unless terminated earlier by mutual written agreement. The obligations in Sections 4, 5, 7, 8, and 9 shall survive termination for an additional **three (3) years** following the Term, **except** that the obligations of confidentiality in respect of any information that constitutes a "trade secret" under applicable law shall continue for as long as the information remains a trade secret.

`[LAWYER REVIEW]` — Some Bangladesh banks ask for a 5-year term as their default. Confirm acceptable range with counsel; the structural number above is conservative.

## 7. Return or destruction

Within fifteen (15) business days of written request by the Discloser, or in any event at the end of the Term, the Recipient shall, at the Discloser's option, return or permanently destroy all Confidential Information in its possession, and shall on request provide written certification of destruction signed by an authorised officer. The Recipient may retain one archival copy as required by its internal records-retention policy or by applicable law, which shall remain subject to the obligations of this Agreement for as long as it is retained.

## 8. No license, no warranty, no obligation to proceed

Nothing in this Agreement grants the Recipient any licence, ownership, or other rights in the Discloser's Confidential Information or intellectual property. All Confidential Information is provided "as is" with no warranty of any kind. Neither Party is obligated by this Agreement to enter into any further agreement or transaction with the other.

## 9. Remedies

The Parties acknowledge that monetary damages may be inadequate to remedy a breach of this Agreement and that the non-breaching Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief in any court of competent jurisdiction without prejudice to any other rights or remedies available at law.

## 10. No publicity

Neither Party shall use the name or marks of the other Party in any public-facing communication (including marketing, press, or website references) without the other Party's prior written consent.

## 11. Governing law and jurisdiction

`[LAWYER REVIEW]` — This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the People's Republic of Bangladesh, and the courts of Dhaka shall have exclusive jurisdiction over any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, save that either Party may seek injunctive relief in any court of competent jurisdiction.

## 12. General

(a) **Entire agreement.** This Agreement is the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter and supersedes any prior or contemporaneous understandings.
(b) **Amendments.** Any amendment must be in writing and signed by authorised representatives of both Parties.
(c) **Assignment.** Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other's prior written consent, except in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all assets, on written notice.
(d) **Severability.** If any provision is unenforceable, the remainder shall continue in full force and effect.
(e) **Counterparts and electronic signature.** This Agreement may be signed in counterparts, including by electronic signature, each of which shall be deemed an original.
